KRATOS Actuals — Data Dictionary
Table: kratos_actuals
Source: FINOPS_Sandbox SQL Server
Rows: 82,937
Description: General ledger actuals and financial planning data exported from the KRATOS financial system. Each row represents a single journal entry line. Used for financial analysis, variance reporting, and AI-assisted queries across all company segments.

=== FISCAL CALENDAR ===
The fiscal year runs July through June. Fiscal period 1 = July, period 12 = June.
Example: FY2026 = July 2025 through June 2026. Current fiscal year is 2026.

=== TRANSACTION IDENTIFIERS ===
ID — Unique row identifier
LINE_UNIQUE_ID — Unique identifier for a single journal entry line
JE_TRANSACTION_NUMBER — Journal entry transaction number in the source accounting system
JE_DOCUMENT_NUMBER — Document reference number associated with the journal entry
JE_NAME — Name or title of the journal entry
JE_MEMO — Primary narrative/description field for the transaction

=== SOURCE & SYSTEM ===
SOURCE — Source system the record originated from (e.g., NetSuite, Sage)
ACCOUNTING_SYSTEM — Name of the accounting system (e.g., NetSuite, Sage Intacct)
DATA_TYPE — Label for a block of transactions (e.g., Actuals, Forecast Snapshot, Budget)
CREATED_BY — User who created the record
CREATED_DATE — Date record was created in the system
MODIFIED_DATE — Date record was last modified
RUN_DATE — Date the data pipeline/cube was last run
FILE_URL — URL to the source file or attachment

=== DATES & FISCAL PERIODS ===
TRX_DATE — Transaction date. Primary date field — use this for all date-based queries.
FISCAL_YEAR — Fiscal year (e.g., 2025 means July 2024–June 2025)
FISCAL_YEAR_QTR — Fiscal year + quarter (e.g., 2025-Q1 = July–September 2024)
FISCAL_PERIOD — Accounting period within the fiscal year. Period 1=July, 2=August, 3=September, 4=October, 5=November, 6=December, 7=January, 8=February, 9=March, 10=April, 11=May, 12=June.
TRX_YEAR — Calendar year of the transaction
TRX_YEAR_QTR — Calendar year + quarter of the transaction
TRX_PERIOD — Calendar period of the transaction

=== FINANCIAL AMOUNTS ===
AMOUNT — Transaction amount in USD. Negative = credit, positive = debit. SUM this column for financial totals.
AMOUNT_ABS — Absolute value of AMOUNT (always positive). Use only when direction does not matter.
ITEM_COUNT — Quantity of items. For Invoice record types, this is the line item quantity.

=== JOURNAL ENTRY CLASSIFICATION ===
JOURNAL_TYPE — Type of journal entry (e.g., Standard, Adjusting, Closing, Accrual)
RECORD_TYPE — NetSuite record type. Values include: Journal, Bill, Invoice, Per Capita. Determines the business context of the transaction.
ITEM_NAME — Line item name. Populated for Invoice record types; represents the product or service billed.
IS_TOPSIDE_JE — 1 if this is a manual topside journal entry (controller-level adjustment outside the normal close process), 0 otherwise
IS_SYSTEM_CALCULATED — 1 if the entry was system-generated rather than manually entered
IS_CAPITALIZED — 1 if the expenditure is capitalized to the balance sheet rather than expensed
IS_IR — 1 if the transaction belongs to the Inside Response business segment

=== ORGANIZATIONAL HIERARCHY ===
DIVISION — Primary business segment. Values (stored uppercase): LIFE, SENIOR, AUTO AND HOME, CORP, ELIMINATION, HEALTHCARE SERVICES, SQAC, ABSI, OTHER. Note: Auto and Home is a single combined division stored as 'AUTO AND HOME'.
SUBDIVISION — Granular breakdown within a division
POD — Operating pod or team unit within a department
SUBSIDIARY_ID — Unique ID of the legal entity
SUBSIDIARY_L1 — Top-level legal entity
SUBSIDIARY_L2 — Second-level subsidiary
SUBSIDIARY_L3 — Third-level subsidiary
SUB_DEPARTMENT_ID — Unique ID of the sub-department
SUB_DEPARTMENT_NAME — Name of the sub-department
DEPARTMENT_NAME — Department name. Primary field for department-level queries.
DEPARTMENT_OWNER_L1 — Top-level department owner name
DEPARTMENT_OWNER_L2 — Second-level department owner
DEPARTMENT_OWNER_L3 — Third-level department owner
USERNAME_L1 — Username of L1 department owner
USERNAME_L2 — Username of L2 department owner
USERNAME_L3 — Username of L3 department owner
LOCATION_ID — Unique ID of the physical or legal location
LOCATION_NAME — Name of the location

=== ACCOUNT CLASSIFICATION ===
ACCOUNT_ID — Unique account ID
ACCOUNT_NUMBER — Chart of accounts number
ACCOUNT_NAME — Chart of accounts description (human-readable account name)
ACCOUNT_TYPE — Account type (e.g., Income, Other Income, Cost of Goods Sold, Other Expense, Expense, Statistical). Use the GAAP hierarchy instead of account_type for financial analysis.
MGMT_L1 — Management reporting hierarchy level 1 (top-level P&L category)
MGMT_L2 — Management reporting hierarchy level 2
MGMT_L3 — Management reporting hierarchy level 3
MGMT_L4 — Management reporting hierarchy level 4 (most granular management view)
GAAP_L1 — GAAP financial statement classification level 1. Known values:
  '01 - GROSS PROFIT' — Revenue and COGS lines
  '02 - OPERATING EXPENSE' — Operating expenses below gross profit
  '03 - NON OPERATING INCOME (EXPENSE)' — Below-the-line non-operating items
  '04 - STATISTICAL' — Statistical/non-financial tracking entries
GAAP_L2 — GAAP classification level 2. Key values:
  '01 - NET REVENUE' — All revenue lines. Use gaap_l2 ILIKE '01 - NET REVENUE' to identify revenue.
  '02 - COGS - SALES' — Cost of goods sold, sales-related
  '03 - COGS - FULFILLMENT' — Cost of goods sold, fulfillment-related
GAAP_L3 — GAAP classification level 3
GAAP_L4 — GAAP classification level 4
GAAP_L5 — GAAP classification level 5 (most granular GAAP view)

=== P&L FLAGS ===
IS_NET_INCOME — 1 if this line flows to Net Income
IS_EBITDA — 1 if this line is included in EBITDA
IS_ADJ_EBITDA — 1 if this line is included in Adjusted EBITDA
IS_606 — 1 if this line is subject to ASC 606 revenue recognition rules
IS_ALLOCATION — 1 if this is an allocated expense (vs a direct charge)
ADJ_EBITDA_ADDBACK_CAPTION — Label for the Adjusted EBITDA addback (e.g., Stock Compensation, Restructuring Charges)

=== ALLOCATIONS ===
ALLOCATION_GROUPING — Grouping name for the allocation pool
ALLOCATION_METHOD — Method used to allocate cost (e.g., headcount, revenue, square footage)
ALLOCATION_TYPE — Type of allocation (e.g., Corporate Overhead, IT Shared Services)

=== SCENARIO / PLANNING ===
SCENARIO_NAME — Scenario label. Values (stored uppercase): ACTUALS, BUDGET, FORECAST. Always filter on SCENARIO_NAME when comparing plan vs actual. Use ILIKE for comparisons: scenario_name ILIKE 'actuals'.
SCENARIO_TYPE — Category of scenario (e.g., Actual, Plan, Forecast)
SCENARIO_DESCRIPTION — Full description of the scenario
CUBE_PROCESSING_FREQUENCY — How often the data cube is refreshed (e.g., Daily, Weekly)
CUBE_PROCESSED_DATE_TIME — Timestamp of the last cube processing run

=== PRODUCT / CARRIER ===
PRODUCT — Product line (e.g., Medicare Supplement, Term Life, Commercial Auto, Homeowners)
CARRIER — Carrier/insurer code
CARRIER_NAME — Name of the insurance carrier

=== VENDOR ===
VENDOR_ID — Unique vendor ID
VENDOR_NAME — Vendor or supplier name
VENDOR_TYPE — Category of vendor (e.g., Carrier, Supplier, Contractor)
VENDOR_DEFAULT_ACCOUNT — Default GL account assigned to this vendor

=== COLUMNS NOT USED IN QUERIES ===

JE_MEMO_ALT, JE_SMART_DESC, DATE, SOURCE_TYPE, ACCOUNT_NUMBER_SAGE, IS_YTD_YTG,
DEPARTMENT_NAME_NS, SUB_CLASS_NAME, SQ_MANUAL_MAP, CLASS_NAME, CUSTOMER_NAME,
PLANNING_TEMPLATE, TEMPLATE_TYPE, SUB_GROUP, EE_LOOKUP, EE_ID, EE_FULL_NAME,
EE_TITLE, EE_QUANTITY, EE_ANNUAL_SALARY, EE_ANNUAL_BONUS_PCT, EE_STATUS,
EE_HIRE_PERIOD, EE_TERM_PERIOD

=== QUERY GUIDANCE FOR AI ===
- To get financial totals, always SUM(amount). Do not use amount_abs unless specifically asked for absolute values.
- Always filter by scenario_name to distinguish Actuals from Budget or Forecast.
- Use trx_date or fiscal_period + fiscal_year for date filtering. Do not use the DATE column.
- The fiscal year runs July–June. Fiscal period 1 = July, period 12 = June.
- Current fiscal year is 2026 (July 2025–June 2026). The only fiscal year in the table is 2026.
- Primary grouping dimensions: division, department_name, account_name, mgmt_l1 through mgmt_l4, gaap_l1 through gaap_l5, product, carrier_name, scenario_name, fiscal_year, fiscal_period.
- Division values (uppercase): LIFE, SENIOR, AUTO AND HOME, CORP, ELIMINATION, HEALTHCARE SERVICES, SQAC, ABSI, OTHER.
- Record types include: Journal, Bill, Invoice, Per Capita.
- is_topside_je = 1 identifies manual controller adjustments — flag these when summarizing actuals.
- is_allocation = 1 identifies allocated (vs direct) costs.
- is_ir = 1 identifies Inside Response segment transactions.

REVENUE IDENTIFICATION (critical):
- Do NOT use account_type to identify revenue. The values 'Income' and 'Other Income' in account_type do NOT reliably capture all revenue.
- Use the GAAP hierarchy instead: gaap_l2 ILIKE '01 - NET REVENUE' to find all revenue lines.
- Example: SELECT SUM(amount::numeric) FROM kratos_actuals WHERE LOWER(scenario_name) = 'actuals' AND fiscal_year::integer = 2026 AND gaap_l2 ILIKE '01 - NET REVENUE'

COGS IDENTIFICATION:
- Use gaap_l2 ILIKE '02 - COGS%' to find all COGS lines (includes both '02 - COGS - SALES' and '03 - COGS - FULFILLMENT').

GROSS PROFIT:
- Gross profit = SUM(amount) WHERE gaap_l1 ILIKE '01 - GROSS PROFIT'

OPERATING EXPENSES:
- Operating expenses = SUM(amount) WHERE gaap_l1 ILIKE '02 - OPERATING EXPENSE'
